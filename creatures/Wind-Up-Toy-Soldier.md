# Wind-Up Toy Soldier

Constructs built by Skabatha Nightshade as static guards for Cinderhold. Each is an oversized tin soldier — jointed, hollow, roughly humanoid — painted in faded red and black and armed with a bayoneted blunderbuss. All of Skabatha's direct creations bear an iron wind-up key protruding from the back. They do not eat, sleep, or tire, and they do not negotiate.

**Deployment at Cinderhold:**
- 2 on the exterior platform (patrol)
- 6 in the entrance / butcher's slab area (static posts)

---

## Wind-Up Toy Soldier

*Small Construct, Unaligned*

**Armor Class** 15 (tin-and-iron casing)
**Hit Points** 18 (4d6 + 4)
**Speed** 25 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 14 (+2) | 10 (+0) | 12 (+1) | 4 (−3) | 10 (+0) | 5 (−3) |

**Damage Immunities** poison, psychic
**Condition Immunities** charmed, exhaustion, frightened, paralyzed, poisoned
**Senses** darkvision 30 ft., passive Perception 10
**Languages** understands Sylvan but cannot speak
**Challenge** 1/2 (100 XP)

---

**Construct Nature.** The soldier doesn't require air, food, drink, or sleep.

**False Stillness.** While motionless and not in combat, the soldier is indistinguishable from an oversized decorative tin figure.

**Wind-Up Key.** A large iron key protrudes from the soldier's back. A creature within 5 feet can use its action to grab and unwind the key (attacker's Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) vs. the soldier's Strength, contested check). On a success, the soldier is incapacitated until any creature uses an action to rewind it.

---

### Actions

**Bayonet.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 5 (1d6 + 2) piercing damage.

**Blunderbuss (Recharge 5–6).** Each creature in a 15-foot cone must make a DC 12 Dexterity saving throw, taking 4 (1d8) piercing damage on a failed save, or half on a success.

---

### Reactions

**Last Wind.** When reduced to 0 hit points, the soldier uses its reaction to make one Bayonet attack or fire its Blunderbuss (if available) before collapsing permanently.

## DM Notes

### Wind-Up Key — Variant Options

The Key Vulnerability above (incapacitate by unwinding) is the default. Consider one or more of the following alternatives or additions:

- **Alert Ratchet** *(swarm synergy)*: When the soldier takes damage, the key clicks audibly (audible to 60 ft.). All other toy soldiers that can hear it and haven't yet acted this round move toward the sound. Rewards the party for isolating soldiers before engaging.

- **Overwound** *(risk/reward)*: A creature can use its action to overwind the key (no check). The soldier enters a frenzied state: speed +10 ft., +2 to attack rolls, takes 1d4 damage at the start of each of its turns. Lasts 1 minute or until the soldier drops. Works for both enemies *and* the party if they capture one.

- **Run Down** *(narrative)*: Without winding by an authorized agent (a master key Skabatha's servants carry), the soldier stops functioning after 24 hours. Soldiers the party bypasses will eventually wind down; servants can reactivate them. Creates a time-pressure alternative to fighting.

### Blunderbuss Positioning Note

The 15-foot cone is especially punishing in the narrow entrance corridor at Cinderhold. If multiple soldiers stack near a choke point, they can cover the entire approach. Consider having two soldiers fan outward while one covers the funnel — forces the party to split the problem.
