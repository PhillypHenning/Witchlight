# Ghost Children — Encounter

*A short encounter on the roads of Thither. Can be placed anywhere between the mushroom circle and Fablerise.*

**The secret the party does not know:** No child has ever escaped Little Treats Orphanage. Jenny and Howard did not escape. They are ghosts — though they do not know it either.

---

## The Hook

One or more party members with a passive Perception of 13 or higher hear it — the rustle of undergrowth, something small scampering away. A moment later, barely hushed whispers from beneath a fallen tree, a child's voice hissing in Common:

> *"Please Jenny, they'll hear you. Please, just a little longer."*

Notable detail for any player paying attention: **Common**. Not Sylvan. Not any fey tongue. Common, spoken by a child, in Thither.

---

## The Children

Huddled in the hollow beneath an uprooted tree are two children. **Howard** (older, maybe ten) sits in front of his sister **Jenny** (seven or eight, clutching his sleeve). When anyone approaches, Howard scrambles to his feet and levels a butter knife at them.

> *"Come any closer to my sister or me and I'll cut yah."*

He means it. His hand is not shaking.

---

## De-escalation

**If the party cannot or does not calm the situation** (or spooks them further): the children bolt into the undergrowth and vanish. Skip to **The Ending**.

**To calm them down:** Any of the following works —
- Persuasion check (DC 12), especially if the player crouches or puts a weapon away
- Gentle approach with no sudden movements — automatic success if the party stays back and speaks softly
- Lindsay's presence as a small fairy may help; Eddie the donkey may help more
- Offering food

Howard does not lower the knife until he decides they are safe. Jenny decides first.

---

## What They Know

Once the party has their trust, the children share the following — Howard speaks, Jenny occasionally adds something he forgot:

1. **Miss Able**
   > *"She's mean during the day. At night she gets— she changes. We don't talk about what she looks like at night."*
   Howard will not elaborate. Jenny shakes her head.

2. **The Toys**
   > *"You can't talk about anything inside. The toys listen. They tell on you. Two kids got taken away because of the toys and they never came back."*
   He doesn't know what the toys are. He knows they move when no one is looking.

3. **The Kidnappers**
   > *"They come through the forest sometimes. They visit the bad things that live out here — they collect for the witch. Always in pairs. Always at night or just before morning."*
   Howard watched them from an upstairs window for a long time. He knows their schedule well enough to have avoided them for days.

4. **Star** *(only if asked about the orphanage or its animals)*
   > *"There's a kitty. Two tails. He's not supposed to be there but Miss Able can't find him — he hides too good. He helps the little ones when they're scared at night. His name is Star."*

---

## The Ending

Whether the party spoke with them or not, the encounter ends the same way. One of the players shifts their weight, steps on a branch, reaches for something — something small, ordinary. A sound. Both children are on their feet instantly, Jenny grabbing Howard's hand. They run.

They are fast. They are gone before anyone can follow. The undergrowth swallows them.

**Perception DC 14:** No snapping branches after the first few steps. No continued rustling. No footfalls. The forest is simply quiet, the way it would be if no one had passed through it at all.

---

## DM Notes

- The children are ghosts; they do not know this and the party should not be certain of it
- They escaped "a few nights ago" and have been dodging kidnappers since — they don't remember exactly how they got out, and look uncertain if pressed
- They cannot be followed; they cannot be found again
- **Star** confirms Dirlagraun's kitten is inside the orphanage — already an outstanding quest for Yanaba; see [Little-Treats-Orphanage.md](../../Worlds/Prismeer/Thither/Locations/Little-Treats-Orphanage.md)
- The Kidnapper intel (pairs, night/pre-dawn timing) is the mechanical payoff; Howard and the butter knife is the scene
- If any player asks whether the children were real afterward: don't answer directly; let it sit
