# Planning

## Recap
The ambush came from three directions at once, and you were not ready for any of them. From the east, creatures that moved like shadows grabbed Yanaba, grappling her, dragging her, refusing to let go. She fought back hard, drew blood, but the second one took her down before she could rise. By the time the smoke cleared, she was gone. Drag marks headed north into the trees. Someone muttered *rabbit folk and the cinder hold* before the dark swallowed her.

From the south, Bullywug soldiers came for Sevro. They called him a false king. They called him a tyrant. He tried to mount their donkey, which didn't go well. The final blow came from a spear—twice—and Sevro died in the dirt of Thither with everything he hadn't finished staying still inside him. Di, he told you not to waste the amulet. He called you a good turtle. He asked you to take care of the bunny, and then he was gone.

From the west, four spirits drifted toward Karag on smoke-dark wings, drawn by the tattoo on his skin like moths to heat. Karag and Seren put them down. After the last spirit fell, when everything was quiet, someone stabbed the Bullywug Knight through the eye. The crown that had been buried in Sevro's chest—embedded there by whatever magic claimed it—came free on the blade. It's rusty. It's bloodied. It sits on a new head now, and the man wearing it said: *Now it's time for revenge.* Karag performed the rites of his faith over Sevro's body. A wash of brown crystalline light moved from Sevro's forehead downward, and the zombie fell away—his true face returned, every scar and wound erased. You made camp against the dead spider, and the forest held its breath around you.

### Yanaba Cut-Scenes (intercut during Beat 2 — DM-narrated, ~1 min each)

Run these as brief flashes at natural pauses during the tracking sequence. Emily is playing Lunzie; these are table-wide glimpses, not player-controlled.

**Cut-Scene 1** — *Shortly after the ambush*
> Canvas. Rough, thick, smelling of rot and iron. She can feel herself moving but can't place direction — just the sway of being carried, the ground too far below, her arms bound behind her. Through a gap near the seam, a sliver: dark branches scrolling past, too fast, the wrong angle for anything walking. Voices near her, low and without inflection, saying nothing she can make out. Her head is heavy. The gap closes. She doesn't remember deciding to sleep.

**Cut-Scene 2** — *Hours later, deeper in the woods*
> She wakes to stillness. The swaying has stopped. Through the canvas she can see light — not daylight, something orange, the color of fire that doesn't move right. Heat, but distant, like a hearth in another room. The iron smell is stronger now. Somewhere close, something groans — long, low, the sound a tree makes before it falls, except it doesn't stop. Her wrists are numb. She tries to shift and a hand presses down on the canvas, once, hard. She goes still. The orange light pulses. The groaning continues.

**Cut-Scene 3** — *Approaching Cinder Hold*
> The canvas pulls tight around her as something lifts — she's being raised. Through the seam: bark. Burning bark, black and cracked, with fire running through the cracks like veins. Iron. The smell is overwhelming now, and it hurts in a way she doesn't have a word for, a wrongness in her blood. Above her, something opens. The groan becomes a creak becomes a grinding of wood and iron and she is carried through. Then darkness. Then the sound of voices — many of them, speaking Sylvan, speaking fast and quiet. And then nothing again.
