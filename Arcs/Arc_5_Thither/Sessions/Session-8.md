# Arc-5, Session-8

# Planning

## Recap

The cinder dunes fought you for every foot of ground. Two separate waves of animated armor and flying swords erupted from the ash, and between them a herd of zombie Harengon sprinted past on some other scent entirely — they never found you. Jinx went down twice, came back up twice, the second time on nothing but stubbornness and a well-timed healing word from Lindsay. Between the second wave and the base of the wall, nobody spoke much.

The climb was twenty stories of corrupted iron-and-wood, handholds and rope, Karag carrying Jinx on their back for the final stretch. When you finally pulled yourselves onto the exterior platform, the entrance spread before you: a double-arched gate of blackened iron, witchfire burning cold in the seam-runes, cells and figures just visible in the dark beyond.

## Beats

1. **The Landing — Root Gate**
   a. A two-door-wide entrance is visible ahead, thick roots knitted around the frame and into the iron — not decorative, structural; the entrance breathes with them
   b. **Without the cape:** Any creature approaching the entrance causes the roots to shift and grow inward, sealing ingress — roots fill the gap completely within one round; the door becomes a wall
   c. **Key asymmetry:** The roots block entry only, not exit — prisoners can't escape, but they could technically leave if they could reach the door; the enchantment cares about getting in
   d. **With the cape worn:** The roots hold still; the entrance stays open
   e. Two toy soldiers stand at the threshold — they are not passive; they snap to attention the moment the party is within 30 feet regardless of the cape
   f. If engaged: standard Blunderbuss cone threat; the corridor funnels the blast (DC 12 DEX save, 1d8 piercing); 90-foot drop to the left if knocked prone near the railing
   g. After combat, searching the soldiers: one has a crumpled Warden's Pass pressed flat inside its tin chest cavity; don't call attention to it — let them find it
   h. See [Wind-Up-Toy-Soldier.md](../../../creatures/Wind-Up-Toy-Soldier.md)

2. **The Interior — Convergence**
   a. The party enters a high-ceilinged space of iron and root; the butcher's slab dominates the center — flat stone, iron runoff channels, dark staining that tells its own story
   b. Six toy soldiers in defensive formation; at least two with Blunderbuss recharged; soldiers near the choke point will fan to cover the approach
   c. **Cut-scene interrupt:** As the first party member enters, Mira and Pip are visible across the space, half-carrying Yanaba toward the far back corner; every Harengon in the cell zones has gone still
   d. **Thorn** stands inside the nearest barred cell, watching the entrance — arms at his sides, not gripping the bars; he's reading the fight, not reacting to it
   e. **Rekt**, in the opposite cell, watches quietly — when the first soldier drops he says, just audibly: *"Those are not Skabatha's people."*
   f. Combat resolves; the prisoners do not intervene
   g. The butcher's slab is what it looks like; don't rush past it
   h. See [Wind-Up-Toy-Soldier.md](../../../creatures/Wind-Up-Toy-Soldier.md)

3. **Thorn Calls Out**
   a. As the last soldier falls — before anyone has a chance to move toward the cells — Thorn's voice comes through the bars, calm and level: *"Who are you? Who sent you?"*
   b. He's not hostile; he's careful; he has seen too many things that looked like rescue turn into something else
   c. Rekt doesn't echo the question; he waits for the answer with his arms folded; he's been in this place long enough to know that the second question matters more than the first
   d. How the party answers sets the tone for everything that follows — let it breathe; don't rush them toward the right answer

4. **The Harengon**
   a. **Leaders:** If the party asks who's in charge, a beat of quiet — then two figures step forward from opposite ends of the cells: the Separatist Leader (young, scarred, tired of looking tired) and the Council Elder (elderly, composed, eyes sharp with things they're not saying)
   b. **Beaten down:** The Harengon as a whole have no fight left; even prisoners who once had the energy to rebel are hollow now; they watch but don't act — not because they can't, but because they've seen what happens when they try
   c. **The door:** It was re-enchanted after the last escape attempt; no amount of force, magic, or cleverness from inside will open it; they know because they tried everything; this isn't despair talking — it's accurate
   d. **Civil war:** Both factions share the space in an uneasy truce held together by exhaustion; the arguments still happen but quieter now; no one starts a fight when they don't have the energy to finish it
   e. **Thorn and Rekt:** Both are willing to go with the party — they'll say so plainly if given the chance; they are not asking permission from anyone in the cells; frame them as potential long-term NPCs; they leave with whatever the party offers and don't look back
   f. **Fablerise:** The older Harengon — those who lived in Yggmir before the fracture, before Cinder Hold — know of Fablerise; not the location exactly, but the weight of the name; *"The silk city"*, one of them might call it; *"Where the spider sits and writes things down that shouldn't be written"*; they know it's in western Thither; they know people go there and come back different, or don't come back at all
   g. **The Demon Lead:** If the party asks about Skabatha's power, or her family, or how someone like her came to have this kind of domain — an elder Harengon who has been here the longest will answer; they will speak quietly, as if the walls still listen:
      - Tasha — Iggwilv — the mother of the coven, the one who made Skabatha what she is — loved demons the way scholars love their books; she wrote the great catalogue of them, every name and shape and binding word; she called it the Demonomicon
      - She had a demon-lover: Graz'zt, Prince of Pleasure, lord of three layers of the Abyss; they were rivals and each other's captors in turns; whether that fire is still burning, no one can say
      - Demons have always had an interest in Prismeer; the Feywild is a place of story and want, and that draws them; some elders believe Graz'zt's eyes have never fully left this plane
      - *"Whatever Skabatha is, she learned it from someone who bargained with the Abyss. That is worth knowing when you decide how to fight her."*

5. **Yanaba's Con Check — Set the Clock**

   Roll at the moment the party reaches Yanaba. The result sets the session's pressure.

   | Roll | Outcome |
   |------|---------|
   | 1–10 | Critical — kidnapper patrol arrives within 1d4+1 hours; the party's window is closing fast |
   | 11–15 | Full long rest required; the party is in the prison overnight |
   | 16–19 | 3–5 hours needed; narrow window to plan and move |
   | 20 | Less than an hour; near-miraculous recovery |

6. **The Reunion**
   a. Yanaba sees Mira — this is Emily's moment; let it happen without narration or interruption
   b. Mira has known since Cut-Scene 1 (Sydney received the private prompt) — she's been holding it
   c. Pip does not know yet; he may have noticed the resemblance before being told; the moment lands here
   d. The father: Mira tells Yanaba, quietly, when there's a pause — *"Your father was two sacrifices ago."* Don't treat this as a beat; let it come out of conversation
   e. Don't move on too quickly; this is the emotional core of the session

7. **Prison Politics**
   a. Thorn gives the lay of the land — factions, sacrifice system, the coup; he's matter-of-fact, slightly tired about all of it; will answer honestly
   b. Rekt (if freed from his side-cell) gives a different account — his framing makes the separatists look brutal and the Council look merely misguided rather than corrupt; he's not lying, but he's curating
   c. Rekt knows the voting was rigged; this is leverage he's holding; he won't share it for free
   d. The sacrifice timeline is the primary ticking clock regardless of which faction is in charge — **set the next date before session**
   e. Let the party ask questions; this is also where the broader horror of the place starts to land (the breeding program, the food chain, the public executions)

8. **The Name That Won't Come**
   a. At the first natural moment someone tries to say the dead bard's name — it won't come
   b. *"You go to say the name and it isn't there. Not forgotten exactly — more like reaching for a word in a language you used to speak. You know there was a name. You know it belonged to someone. It's gone."*
   c. Di's name: same, if anyone tries; "Patunia" / "Pete" still all they have
   d. Jinx being new to the group may trigger this naturally — he might ask about the person he replaced
   e. Don't force it; wait for the organic moment
