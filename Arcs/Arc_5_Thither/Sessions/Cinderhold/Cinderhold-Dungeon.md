# Cinder Hold — Dungeon Planning

**Companion to**: [Yanaba-in-Cinderhold.md](./Yanaba-in-Cinderhold.md)
**World Reference**: [Cinder-Hold-Prison.md](../../../../Worlds/Prismeer/Thither/Locations/Cinder-Hold-Prison.md)

The prison is a living, moving structure — a corrupted ancient treant named Yggmir, wreathed in witchfire. There are no wardens or guards. The tree itself is the jailer. Interior spaces shift as the treant walks; what was north yesterday may be west today.

---

## Exterior — The Approach

### What They Hear First
Cinderhold announces itself long before it appears. Layer these as the party moves north:

1. **Underfoot** — a low, rhythmic tremor in the earth. Not an earthquake. Too regular. Like a heartbeat, but wrong. Felt in the soles of the feet, then the knees.
2. **On the wind** — the groan of iron-stressed wood, deep and ongoing, like a ship in a storm that never ends. Beneath it, if the wind shifts: something that might be crying. Or might be the tree itself.
3. **On the horizon** — a glow. Orange-green, flickering but constant. Witchfire doesn't behave like firelight; it doesn't pulse or dance. It just *is*, steady and wrong, visible from miles away in the dark of Thither's canopy.
4. **The smell** — char and sap and something metallic. The air tastes like the inside of a forge.

### The Scorched Loop
Cinderhold does not wander randomly. It walks a fixed circuit — a slow, endless patrol of rolling hills in northern Thither. The path it has carved over years is unmistakable:

- A ring of scorched earth, 30–40 feet wide, stripped of all growth
- The ground within the ring is cracked and compressed from repeated passes
- Witchfire residue leaves the soil faintly luminescent at night
- The circuit takes Cinderhold roughly **1.5 hours** to complete

**Tactical implication**: The party can find the ring, read its direction of travel from fresh scorch marks and the depth of recent impressions, and choose where to wait or intercept.

### First Sight — Read-Aloud
> The trees thin, and then there are no trees. The canopy ends at a hard line, as if the forest decided it wanted nothing to do with what lives beyond. Ahead, the hills are open and black — not night-dark but *burnt* dark, the color of things that used to be alive. And at the far end of the valley, moving slowly against the sky, is the reason why.
>
> It is taller than anything has a right to be. A treant — or what was one — walking on roots that tear free of the ground with each step and slam back down like hammers. Every surface is burning: not consuming, not collapsing, just burning, wrapped in green-orange witchfire from root to crown. Iron bands circle its trunk at intervals, bolted through the bark. Where windows might have been, boards and thorns seal every opening. It groans as it moves. Not in pain, or not only in pain. In the way that old prisons groan — full.

### Detection — The 300-Foot Radius
Cinderhold's corrupted consciousness extends through the ground around it. Any creature that comes within **300 feet** without Skabatha's permission is detected instantly.

**The response is not subtle:**
- Roots erupt from the earth within the detection radius
- Massive clods of dirt and stone are hurled outward — not precise, but each one is the size of a cart
- **Ranged attack**: +8 to hit, 3d10 bludgeoning, target is knocked prone on a hit
- Cinderhold does not pursue beyond its circuit path; it simply makes the 300-foot radius very unpleasant until intruders leave or die

**The ground itself is the warning**: within 300 feet, the soil is warm underfoot, and alert players (Perception DC 13) notice the roots shifting just below the surface — coiling, ready — before Cinderhold reacts.

### The Kidnappers — Guaranteed Sighting
While the party observes from beyond the 300-foot line, two of Skabatha's Kidnappers approach Cinderhold openly.

**What the party sees:**
- Two hunched figures walking straight toward the treant without slowing
- The roots do not erupt. The ground does not move.
- A section of the lower trunk shifts — bark and iron peeling back like a mouth opening — and the Kidnappers walk inside
- The opening seals behind them. The treant continues its circuit as if nothing happened.

**What this teaches:**
- Some creatures are permitted entry
- Permission is not a physical key — the treant *recognizes* them
- The question the party now has to answer: how do you become something Cinderhold recognizes?

**DM Note**: Don't explain the mechanic. Let the players sit with it. They saw it happen. They can theorize.

### Entry — The Puzzle
The party cannot fight their way in. Options to surface through play, not exposition:

| Approach | What's Required | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Skabatha's mark or token | Obtaining something that carries her authority | Getting close to Skabatha |
| Shadowing the Kidnappers | Timing entry behind them before the trunk seals | Extremely tight window; Kidnappers will notice |
| Yggmir's cooperation | Establishing contact with the tree's buried consciousness | Long-term; multiple sessions |
| Creative deception | Convincing Cinderhold they belong (via magic, disguise, or something stranger) | DM's discretion |

## Dungeon Principles

- **No conventional guards** — the treant senses movement and crushes escape attempts
- **Witchfire everywhere** — burns but never consumes; provides eerie orange-green light
- **Cold iron** — threaded through wood, cages, floors; agonizing for fey prisoners
- **Constant motion** — floors tilt, passages narrow, roots shift; unsettling at all times
- **No teleportation** — wards prevent magical escape
- **Sound carries** — prisoners in different sections can hear but not always see each other

### Zone 1 — The Intake Chamber
*Where new prisoners arrive. Branches close behind them like a fist.*

- Iron shackle-hooks embedded in the walls; most empty, some with old restraints
- A rough table covered in confiscated items (weapons, tools, meaningful objects)
- A narrow root-corridor leads deeper into the tree

**Key NPC**: [Thorn](../../../../../Worlds/Prismeer/Thither/NPCs/Thorn.md) — Separatist enforcer; manages arrivals, keeps a log on bark-paper

---

### Zone 2 — The General Cages
*The main living area. Iron and bramble cages arranged in a rough circle around a central open space.*

- 8–12 cages of varying size; most hold 2–4 prisoners
- The central space is the "commons" — where arguments, meals, and faction tensions play out
- Witchfire sconces at irregular heights; shadows everywhere
- Prisoners from both factions share this space; tension is constant and close

**Key Feature — The Sacrifice Slab**: A flat section of treant-wood at the center of the commons, stained dark. No one sits near it. Everyone knows what it's for.

---

### Zone 3 — The Locked Ward
*Where the Separatists jailed the Council after the coup. Older Harengon, cramped conditions.*

- Heavier iron work; cages pressed closer together
- Separatist hardliners patrol this area more than anywhere else
- Evidence of the rigged voting may still exist somewhere in here — records on bark-paper, hidden in an old Council member's bedding

**Key NPC**: [Reck](../../../../../Worlds/Prismeer/Thither/NPCs/Reck.md) *(file TBD)* — elderly Council loyalist; watching, waiting, calculating

### Zone 4 — The Swings
*The upper branches. Isolated prisoners held here for crimes against the prison population itself.*

The tree's movement is most pronounced at this height — the floor tilts, the walls groan, and everything sways in long slow arcs with each of Cinderhold's steps. Prisoners here are caged individually, well out of earshot from the zones below.

### Zone 5 — The Root Tunnels
*Below the main structure. Roots shift here constantly; passages appear and disappear.*

- Where the Kidnappers enter to collect the weekly sacrifice — they know a route; no one else does
- The only non-magical potential way out of Cinder Hold
- Possibly: a section of the tunnels connects to fragments of Yggmir's original consciousness — the world tree's buried self, still present, still confused
- Navigating without the Kidnappers' knowledge requires either following them or luck (DC 17 Survival)

## Party Events

### Event 1 — The Moderate's Request
A middle-aged Harengon prisoner — neither Separatist nor Council — finds a moment to speak with the party alone. She has spent weeks watching both sides and has no loyalty left for either. She knows how the Kidnappers enter and where they go in the Root Tunnels. She will share this freely — but only after the party retrieves the rigged-voting records from the Locked Ward and brings them to her. She is not asking for rescue. She wants the truth in her hands before anything else happens.

**What she offers**: The Kidnapper route through Zone 5 — the only confirmed exit
**What she wants**: The bark-paper records from Zone 3
**Complication**: Getting into Zone 3 means going past Separatist hardliner patrols — and past Reck

---

### Event 2 — Reck's Offer
From behind the bars of the Locked Ward, an elderly Harengon with careful eyes watches the party enter Zone 3. He doesn't shout. He waits until they're close, then speaks quietly.

He knows where a hidden cache of confiscated prisoner gear is — weapons, tools, items taken from prisoners over years of arrivals. He won't say where until he's moved to general population in Zone 2. Not escaped. Not freed. Just out of the ward.

**What he offers**: Location of confiscated gear cache (useful for escape)
**What he wants**: Transfer to Zone 2 — general population
**Complication**: Moving Reck means dealing with Thorn, who will want to know why. It also means Reck is loose in the commons, which will cause problems with both factions.

---

### Event 3 — The Voice from the Swings
If the party passes through Zone 4, one of the cages holds someone who doesn't belong there. Not violent. Not a threat to anyone. A young Harengon — barely more than a child — locked up here alone because she witnessed something she wasn't supposed to and the Separatist leadership decided silence was safer than trust.

She doesn't call out until she's sure they're not guards. Then she does, quietly: *"Please. I'm not supposed to be up here. I haven't done anything."*

She cannot get herself out. She is too small to force the cage and too high up to survive a drop. She is simply asking.

**What she offers**: Nothing, at first. If rescued and given time to breathe, she knows the prison's layout in detail — she's been here long enough, and small enough, to have noticed things others missed. She also knows roughly when the next sacrifice is scheduled.
**What she wants**: Out of the cage. Out of The Swings. She's not asking to leave the prison — she knows that's not possible. She just doesn't want to die up here alone.
**Complication**: Getting her down means carrying her through Zones the party may not have cleared. She's also terrified of heights, which makes the swaying worse. She will not be quiet about it.

---

## Escape Conditions

Cinder Hold cannot be escaped by force. Options:
1. **The Kidnapper Route** — through Zone 5; requires the Moderate's information or the Kidnappers themselves
2. **Skabatha's Permission** — impossible unless the party has serious leverage over her
3. **Yggmir's Cooperation** — extremely long-term; requires multiple sessions and real relationship-building with the tree's buried consciousness
4. **Outside Help** — the main party arrives with a plan; this is the expected endgame

---

## DM Notes

- The dungeon should feel alive and hostile even when nothing is attacking
- Use the tree's movement as a pressure mechanic: plans that work now may not work in an hour
- The Kidnappers arrive on a schedule — use this as a ticking clock
- The two events (Moderate and Reck) are designed to overlap: helping one leads directly into the territory of the other
- Yggmir fragments are a slow burn, not a solution; don't let players think they can befriend their way out in one session
