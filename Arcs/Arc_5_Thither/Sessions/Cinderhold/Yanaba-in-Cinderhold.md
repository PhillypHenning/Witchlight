# Yanaba in Cinder Hold — One-Off Session Planning

**Format**: Emily plays Yanaba. Other players each take a pre-gen character already inside the prison. Each player gets 1–2 paragraphs of character context and a personal goal.

## Pre-Gen Characters

### Mira — Yanaba's Mother (Sydney)
Mira has been inside Cinder Hold for years — long enough that she no longer counts the days. She was a healer in the old community, known for a steady hand and a refusal to panic. The prison has not broken her, but it has changed her. She speaks slowly now, measures everything, trusts almost no one. She is aware of the Harengon politics and has kept her head down through both regimes, which has made her enemies on both sides.

**The Stork:** In Prismeer, storks carry newborns between families. Mira's stork was sent for Yanaba before the imprisonment — and never came back. The family knows only that the stork went missing; they have no information about what happened to the child it was carrying. Mira does not know if her daughter is alive or dead.

**The Recognition (CS-1):** Mira will recognize Yanaba while treating her wound in Cut-Scene 1 — the shape of her face, the notch in her ear. This moment is delivered to Sydney in private (whispered aside or direct message). Sydney then decides what Mira does with that knowledge. See [Cut-Scene-1.md](./Cut-Scene-1.md) for the full private prompt.

**Goal**: Keep Yanaba alive and find a way out, even if it means making deals she'll regret.

---

### Pip — Yanaba's Little Brother (Travis)
Pip was born inside Cinder Hold. He has never seen a sky that wasn't filtered through iron and witchfire. He's scrappy, loud when he thinks he can get away with it, and deeply loyal to the few people he trusts. He idolizes Mira and is terrified of the Separatists, though he'd never admit either.

**What Pip knows:** He does not know about Yanaba. He has heard Mira say a girl's name quietly, sometimes, when she thinks he's asleep — but he has no context for it and has never asked directly.

**Perception Check (DC 13):** When Yanaba wakes in CS-1, Pip can make a Perception check to notice the family resemblance — the shape of her face, the cut of her jaw — before being told anything. On a success, he notices but doesn't know what to do with it. On a failure, she's just a new arrival until something else triggers it.

**Goal**: Stick close to the new arrival and figure out who she is.

---

### Thorn — Separatist Goon (Cas)
Thorn backed the coup because he watched three people his age die in "fair" votes while the old guard kept surviving. He's not cruel — he's tired and angry and convinced he's finally on the right side of something. He serves the Separatist leadership as a muscle-and-loyalty man: keeps order, escorts prisoners, enforces decisions.

He doesn't love what the Separatists are doing to the elderly. He tells himself it's necessary. When Yanaba arrives as a new prisoner, he's the one tasked with processing her and explaining how things work in here. He has more doubt than he shows.

**Goal**: Do his job, keep the peace, and try not to think too hard about whether this is justice.

---

### Reck — Council Loyalist Goon (Dan)
Reck is elderly by Harengon standards, old enough to have served the Council before the coup. He's jailed now, which means he spends his days listening, watching, and waiting. He was never on the Council himself — he was their enforcer, same as Thorn is now for the other side. He knows more than he lets on, including the truth about the rigged voting, which he helped cover up.

He's not a good person, but he's a pragmatic one. If a new prisoner shows up with the potential to shift the balance of power, Reck will find a way to introduce himself. He'll frame it as offering help. He wants out of his cell. What he does with freedom after that is a separate question.

**Goal**: Get out of the jailed section. Use the new arrival to do it if possible.

## Session Structure

### Entry Beat
- Yanaba regains consciousness inside a moving cage — the tree groans and shifts around her
- Pip is the first face she sees (he noticed the new arrival before Mira did)
- Mira arrives shortly after, composed, then not

### Middle Beats
- Introduction to prison factions — Thorn gives the "official" rundown; Reck offers a different version
- Discovery of the weekly sacrifice timeline — next one is soon
- Political pressure: both sides want Yanaba to pick a side or be useful
- Possible discovery: evidence of rigged voting exists somewhere in the prison

### Climax Options
- The sacrifice is called early — someone must be chosen
- An escape attempt is made by one faction, threatening everyone
- Yanaba learns something about how to get out (or in) that the main party can use later

### Handoff to Main Party
- Intel Yanaba can pass to the party: patrol timing, Cinder Hold's movement pattern, a weakness or entry point
- What she promises to the people she's leaving behind (if she escapes)

---

## DM Notes
- Tone: oppressive but not hopeless — Yanaba should feel the weight but have something to hold onto
- Mira and Pip should feel like real people, not just quest objectives
- Thorn is a potential ally; Reck is a potential tool — neither is simply good or bad
- The rigged voting reveal is optional; use it if the session needs a turning point
- Don't resolve the faction war — leave it messy for the main party to inherit
