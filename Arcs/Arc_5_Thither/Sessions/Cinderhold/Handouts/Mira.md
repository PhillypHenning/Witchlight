# Mira — Player Handout
**Played by: Sydney**

---

You are Mira. You have been inside Cinder Hold long enough that you stopped counting years and started counting other things: favors owed, favors called, names of the dead, things still worth protecting.

You were a healer in the community before the coup. You are still a healer, because being useful keeps you alive, and keeping yourself alive is how you keep other people alive. The Separatists tolerate you. The Loyalists remember when you weren't their enemy. You survive by being the thing everyone needs and nobody wants to spend resources on.

**You know Thorn.** He's Separatist muscle — young, not cruel, more certain than he should be about which side of history he's on. He calls you when someone needs patching up and the leadership has decided they're worth it. You patch them up. You ask no questions. He nods to you in the corridors. You nod back. You have a knock relay set up — three prisoners on the south side pass the signal. Simple. Reliable enough.

**You know Reck.** He's in Zone 3 now — the Locked Ward, where the Separatists put the old Council's people after the coup. You knew him before: he was the Council's enforcer, the one who made problems disappear quietly. You were never friends. You treated him once, years ago, and he thanked you by pretending it never happened, which suited you both. He's smart and he's patient and he wants something from everyone he talks to. When he calls from the bars, it means he's already decided you're useful.

**What you carry:** Before you came here, a stork was sent for your daughter. In Prismeer, that's how it works — the storks carry children between families. Yours didn't come back. No word, no body, no answer. You don't know if she was lost in transit, taken, or simply never arrived. You have thought about it every day for years without resolution, and you have learned to think about it quietly, in the moments before sleep, so it doesn't get in the way of the work.
