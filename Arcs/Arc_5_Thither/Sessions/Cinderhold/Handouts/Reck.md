# Reck — Player Handout
**Played by: Dan**

---

You are Reck. You are old — old enough that you remember when the Council was new, when the votes were contested and the outcomes were not yet certain. You were never on the Council yourself. You were the kind of person Councils need but don't talk about: the one who made problems go away quietly. You were good at it. You are still good at it, even from behind bars.

The coup put you in the Locked Ward — Zone 3, heavier iron, tighter cages, Separatist hardliners on patrol. They jailed you because they know what you are. They're not wrong about that. What they miscalculated is that being jailed doesn't make you less dangerous. It just changes your tools.

You spend your days the way you've always spent them: watching and waiting. You know the patrol timing in Zone 3 well enough to recite it backwards. You know which Separatist guards are certain and which ones have doubt behind their eyes. You know things about the rigged voting that could burn both sides of this faction war — things you helped engineer and haven't told anyone, because information held is information worth something.

**You know Mira.** She was the community healer before the coup — kept herself useful to whoever was in power, which is a strategy you respect. She treated you once, years ago, and neither of you mentioned it afterwards. She's still operating the same way in here: patch people up, ask no questions, keep her head down. She's smarter than she lets on and she has access that you don't. That makes her interesting.

**What you can see:** The Zone 3 bars face Zone 2 at an angle. When something happens in the commons — a new arrival, a confrontation, anything that draws a crowd — you can see it. You can be heard if you raise your voice.

**Your goal:** Get yourself moved out of Zone 3 and into general population. Not escaped — just out of this ward. You can work with the rest from there. The new arrival looks like the kind of lever that might move something.

---

*What you know that nobody else does: you know where a cache of confiscated prisoner gear is hidden — tools, items taken from arrivals over years of intake. You've been saving this information for the right moment. This might be it.*
