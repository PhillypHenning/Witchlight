## Part 1 — The Drop
*DM narrates. No player control yet. Read aloud.*

> Your world is heat and fragments. Cold stone against your cheek. Voices — close, too close — speaking words that won't resolve into meaning. Shapes above you. The light is the wrong colour.
>
> Your face is on fire. Not heat, exactly — something deeper than heat. A wet, searing line from your cheekbone down to your jaw, throbbing in time with your pulse. You try to raise your hand. Your body refuses. Somewhere far away, or very close, something drips.
>
> The ceiling is iron and stone. A cage. You are inside a cage. There are sounds you recognise now — breathing, movement, the low creak of metal. The sounds of other people.
>
> Then your eyes close, and the room goes away.

*End the narration there. Do not allow player input during this sequence.*

## Part 2 — Player Control Begins
*Thorn is on patrol near the intake end of Zone 2 when the heavy iron intake door opens. A body hits the floor. The door seals. This is routine. This particular body is not.*

**What Thorn sees:**
- Harengon female, young adult
- Deep laceration from cheekbone to jaw — deliberate, not a fight wound; clean line, wrong angle for a struggle
- Breathing, barely
- No possessions. Whatever she had was taken before she came in.

**Thorn's call:** Protocol says report to the leader and wait for processing. But the blood is spreading on the stone floor, and she's Harengon, and having a dead intake creates more problems than it solves. He knows where to find a healer.

*If Cas hesitates or asks what the normal procedure is:* The blood will reach the cage bars within the minute. The Separatist leadership will ask what happened to the new prisoner. Thorn will need an answer.

**Sending the signal:** The knock relay — three prisoners on the south side, passed hand to hand. Mira has the same system. She'll arrive within 5–10 minutes.

## Part 3 — Mira's Hands

*Mira arrives. She has her kit — limited supplies, what she can scrounge. She assesses the wound.*

*Mira does not yet know this is her daughter. She sees a badly hurt Harengon girl.*

### Medicine Check (Sydney rolls)

| Result | Outcome |
|--------|---------|
| **18+** | The wound closes cleanly. Mira's hands are steady and the conditions cooperate, barely. A fine scar will remain — the kind that looks like history, not disaster. Yanaba will wake looking like someone who survived something. |
| **13–17** | Decent work under difficult conditions. The bleeding stops. The wound closes, but unevenly — it will heal prominent, a raised ridge from cheekbone to jaw. Yanaba wakes with the scar as a permanent feature of her face. |
| **8–12** | The tools available aren't enough. The wound is bound, the worst of the bleeding slowed, but it will heal ragged. When Yanaba wakes, her vision on that side blurs at the edges. A lasting quirk, or something worse — DM decides. |
| **Under 8** | Mira does what she can. The wound is cleaned and wrapped, but something has gone wrong she can't fix here. The scar will be severe. One eye may be permanently affected. *DM decides the lasting consequence before next session.* |

### The Recognition — Private Message to Sydney

*Send this out of band — whispered aside, written note, or direct message. Do not read aloud to the table.*

> As Mira works — cleaning the cut, pressing the edges together, trying to assess the bone beneath — she tilts the girl's face toward the witchfire light to see the wound clearly.
>
> And then she goes very still.
>
> The shape of the cheekbone. The set of the brow. The small notch on the upper left ear — she used to worry at it with her thumb when she nursed her, in the days before the stork came. Before the stork didn't come back.
>
> She knows.
>
> She finishes the work. Her hands don't shake. She is very proud of that.

*Sydney: Mira now knows this is Yanaba. She doesn't tell Thorn. She doesn't tell anyone in the room. What she does with this information is entirely up to you.*

## Part 4 — The Watch
*Time skip. Narrate briefly:*

> She didn't wake that night. Mira came back twice to change the dressing. Pip came back more than that.

*When Yanaba's eyes open, Pip is there. He's been stationed nearby for most of the last day — positioned close to her section of the cage on the pretense of doing something else. He is not subtle about it.*

### Pip's Perception Check (Travis rolls, DC 13)

**Success:** Something tugs at him — the curve of her ear, the line of her jaw. He's seen that somewhere. In Mira's face. Possibly in his own. He doesn't say anything yet. He doesn't know what to say. He stares at her a beat too long before catching himself.

**Failure:** She's just a new arrival. He's friendly, curious in the way of someone who's never had many new things to look at, but there's no recognition. Not yet.

**Either way:** Pip is the first face Yanaba sees. He introduces himself. He tells her, with the casual confidence of someone born in this place, how things work here — who the factions are, what the slab in the middle of the room is for, what you do and don't say around which people.

## Part 5 — The Leader's Questions
*Thorn and Mira return together with the leader.*

### Leader Name Suggestions
- **Yavel** — Sharp, vowel-heavy, fey-sounding. A name chosen rather than given.
- **Drast** — Hard consonants, older feel. Someone who survived the old Council.
- **Corra** — Could be male or female; sounds like something that corrals and contains.

### The Entrance
The leader doesn't rush in. They stand in the entrance for a moment and look at Yanaba the way you look at a problem before you've decided what kind of problem it is. Then they enter. Thorn flanks them. Mira stays near the back.

### Question Sequence
1. What's your name?
2. Where were you before you were taken?
3. What were you doing in Thither?
4. *(If answers are vague or evasive)* You were brought in by Kidnappers. The Kidnappers work for Skabatha. Why does Skabatha want you in Cinder Hold?

### The Leader's Tells
- Wary, not hostile. There is a difference.
- They watch Mira's face during Yanaba's answers. They notice things, and Mira knows they notice things.
- If Yanaba mentions outside agency — the staff, a party, any mission — the leader goes very still. That information has value.

### End Condition
Once satisfied (or satisfied enough), the leader gives the order: she's processed, she's housed in Zone 2, she's not to be bothered until she's on her feet. Then they leave. Thorn and Mira may linger.

*This is a natural pause point. Give the players a moment before moving into the dungeon proper.*

### Reck's Opening — From the Bars
*Once the leader has gone and Thorn has stepped away, a voice comes from the direction of Zone 3 — low, unhurried, not shouting. Just loud enough.*

> "That's quite a scar. Kidnappers don't usually bother with the face. Must have made someone angry."

*Reck is visible at the Zone 3 bars — an old Harengon, gripping the iron with both hands, watching. He's been watching since she came in.*

**If anyone approaches or responds:** He's friendly in the way of someone who has nothing but time and is very good at using it. He doesn't ask for anything yet. He just talks — mentions he's seen a lot of arrivals, mentions the slab, mentions that new prisoners usually have a grace period before the first vote. He's orienting her. Helpfully.

**His actual read:** He's assessing Yanaba for leverage potential. How she responds to pressure, who she looks at when she's uncertain, whether she's the kind of person who makes deals or the kind who thinks she doesn't have to. He won't make his ask this scene. He's planting the seed.

*Dan: Reck is not playing the long game out of patience — he's playing it because he's been here long enough to know that rushing gets you selected. Don't tip your hand. Just be useful.*

## The Floor — Location

*Zone 2, as Yanaba sees it when she's well enough to look.*

Cinder Hold's general population lives in a single vast chamber. Iron cages run along every wall in a continuous linked chain — floor-to-ceiling bars, thick-wrought and old, forming one long unbroken ring that curves with the room. There are no individual cells; prisoners move within sections at the discretion of whoever holds the keys.

At the center of the room, set directly into the treant-wood floor, is the **Sacrifice Slab**: flat pale stone, roughly three meters long, stained so deeply with old blood that the middle has gone almost black. Gutters ring it, worn smooth, leading to iron drains. The slab is always clean. Someone keeps it very clean. Prisoners do not sit near it. Nobody explains why to newcomers. They figure it out.

This is where the Kidnappers take prisoners who have been selected. The process is methodical. Feet first. The meat is bagged and collected at a hatch near the intake door. It goes to Little Treats Orphanage.

Witchfire burns in iron sconces at irregular heights, casting everything in orange-green light the color of old bruises. There are no windows. There is no sky.

### Read-Aloud (first time Yanaba sees the room properly)

> The room is large enough that the far wall is dim. Iron cages wrap every surface in a continuous chain — floor-to-ceiling bars, thick and dark, running the full circuit of the room as though the walls have grown teeth. There are figures in them. Most are still. At the center of the floor, clear of any cage, is a long flat stone. It might once have been pale. It isn't anymore. Channels run from it toward the floor. Something is always being cleaned away. Above, witchfire burns in iron sconces — orange-green, steady and cold, the color of things that used to be alive. There are no windows. There is no sky.

## Supporting NPCs

### Renne — Child of the Floor
*DM-controlled.*

Renne is nine, or close to it. She was born inside Cinder Hold and has never seen open sky. She is scrappy, observant, and deeply attached to Pip — the closest thing she has to an older sibling. She knows Zone 2 the way only someone who has spent years crawling through its gaps can: which cage links have worked loose, which guards leave early, where to press flat and be invisible.

She is not naive. She has seen the slab used. She keeps moving.

Renne will notice Yanaba before most others do, but she won't approach directly. She watches from a distance — reading her for threat, for use, for possibility — before she decides anything.

**Use her for:** In-world intel delivery to players, introducing escape route possibilities (she knows a gap into Zone 5 that adults can't fit through but she can), emotional grounding, and raising the stakes if anyone threatens her.

---

### Haleth — The Survivor
*DM-controlled.*

Haleth doesn't have a faction. Haleth has outlasted four of them. They're old — whip-cord thin, fur gone mostly grey — and they move through Zone 2 like smoke: always present, never bothered, somehow never selected. Both factions leave Haleth alone. Nobody's quite sure why, which is exactly how Haleth wants it.

Haleth knows things: which leader has leverage on which, where something is buried in the floor of the cage section near the east wall, and — crucially — a detail about Cinderhold's movement cycle that predates the current leadership by decades. They will not share any of it without a price. Their price is their own freedom. They have been told this is impossible. They are waiting, with great patience, for someone to disagree.

**Use them for:** Backstory and political context delivered slowly, alliance-building that requires real trust, and as a wildcard in any escape sequence. Haleth's knowledge about the buried thing is a thread the DM can pick up or leave.
