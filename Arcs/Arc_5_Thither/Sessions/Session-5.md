# Arc-5, Session-5

# Planning

## Recap
You finally got them out. Karag found Tilly Tumbleweed still riding her winning streak at the dice tables—giddy, generous, and utterly unaware of the gilded trap she'd wandered into. A few quiet words were enough: *the house always wins.* She cashed out ahead, bought herself a bag of holding as a memento, and walked out with her head held high. Yanaba and Seren found Grimble in worse shape—locked in a bathroom stall, crying quietly, twenty-three stravens in the hole and ready to pay the only way he had left. You gave him what you could, told him the Witchlight still wanted him, and watched the misery drain from his face. He left without looking back.

Sevro's evening went differently. He went upstairs with two charming escorts and drank a potion without asking what it did—it made him look alive, for a while. He felt nothing. The escorts did not appreciate the shortage of payment, and a man named Gorax made his feelings known with a pair of brass knuckles. Sevro came downstairs lighter by fifteen stravens and one intact nose. Marlon was the one you couldn't reach: a half-elf who'd gone into the high-roller room with hope and come out a decade older, stripped of everything and bound by contract to fifteen years of service. He told you to pass along his regards to Mr. Witch and Mr. Light. You left him there.

Caldrien found Seren before you left. The Never Leave Spa—the casino's chief competition—was a problem he'd very much like to stop being a problem. He smiled when he said it. You collected your weapons at the door, got directions from a security guard—west to a river, then south—and pushed into the evening. Maurnu had caught you outside the casino earlier; Tempest was at the Toyhouse, she said, which sits inside the orphanage, which is Skabatha's home. She hoped your goals would align someday soon. The march was harder than expected. When Sevro woke to make breakfast, he tried to say Di's name—and the word wasn't there. None of you could find it. He offered "Patunia." Di seemed unbothered, almost free. You made camp beneath the molted shell of a massive spider, Seren winding silk from its legs while the torchlight kept the maggots at bay. That was when Yanaba heard it: a snap, something fast in the dark. Two pairs of yellow eyes from the east. Four figures closing from the south. Four black-cloaked shapes drifting toward Karag from the west, trailing darkness like smoke. You were not alone.

## Beats

1. **3-Way Ambush** — Dark Thither woods, camp at the spider molt ([3-Way-Ambush.md](../3-Way-Ambush.md))
   a. **Sevro vs. Downfall Challengers** — Knight & Squire approaching from the south
      - Knight opens with formal declaration: *"I claim your crown, false king! Face me, mad tyrant!"*
      - Knight wants single combat; Squire flanks and interferes if Sevro is winning
      - Will not target other party members unless directly intervened
      - If Knight falls: Squire retreats to report back
   b. **Yanaba vs. Skabatha's Kidnappers** — Two pairs of yellow eyes from the east
      - Capture, not kill — nets, grapples, restraints; non-lethal on Yanaba, lethal on interferors
      - If captured: *"The Night Shade wants the rabbit-child. The family will be reunited in Sindrhald."*
      - If repelled: party learns Skabatha is actively hunting all free Harengon
   c. **Karag vs. Twilight Spirits** — Four ethereal black-cloaked figures from the west ([3-Way-Ambush.md](../3-Way-Ambush.md))
      - Curse tattoo burns with intense pain as spirits approach — beacon is active
      - Spirits fixated on Karag; fragmented whispers: *"Heir..." "What remains..." "Remember us..."*
      - Vulnerability to radiant damage (gives Karag's paladin abilities a meaningful role)
      - Spirits pause one round if Karag speaks words of recognition ("I am the Heir") — DM discretion
      - Spirits dissipate rather than die; may leave a Twilight Shard
   d. **Seren's Compulsion** — Wisdom save DC 13 to resist throwing herself between Karag and the spirits
      - No clear reason — purely instinctual; visible to the party regardless of outcome
   e. **Post-Battle Reveals**
      - Knight loot: sealed letter from Downfall, Kagg's token or ring, written commission
      - Kidnapper loot: map fragment toward Cinder Hold, Skabatha's written orders
      - Spirit loot: Twilight Shard (dim glowing fragment — DM to define use)
      - Captured Knight confirms: more will come; the crown must change hands

   b. **Morning** — Di still nameless; "Patunia" / "Pete" stands; party continues west

3. **Travel to Never Leave Spa** — West to river, then south following the river ([Never-Leave-Spa.md](../../../Worlds/Prismeer/Thither/Locations/Never-Leave-Spa.md))
   a. Constitution checks if pushing travel pace
   b. Random encounters possible ([Random-Encounters.md](../Random-Encounters.md))

      - *"What is that sound?"* — foreshadow before arrival
   d. Building appears: low marble walls, fogged-glass windows glowing warmly, scented steam from chimneys
      - Sign by the door: *"Rest, relaxation, and the finest baths in all Thither."*

4. **Never Leave Spa** ([Never-Leave-Spa.md](../../../Worlds/Prismeer/Thither/Locations/Never-Leave-Spa.md))
   a. **The Song of Forgetting** — DC 16 Wisdom save; failure means the False Hydra cannot be targeted
      - Deafened creatures are immune — earplugs, Silence spell, or self-inflicted deafness bypass the song
      - Hearing protection reveals horror immediately: attendants are pale eyeless heads on necks, NPCs screaming
   b. **Spa Rooms**
      - *Entrance Hall*: marble floors, empty reception desk, guest book with dissolving names
      - *Main Bath Chamber*: large mineral pool, thick fog, shadowy "attendants" guiding guests in
      - *Massage Chambers (x3)*: scented oil, low tables, drains dark with old blood
      - *Steam Room*: source of the mist; brass pipes feeding thick steam — key to clearing the fog
      - *Private Pools (x4)*: individual copper tubs; several occupied by "guests"
   c. **NPC Guests** — relaxed, dreamy-eyed, sporting terrible injuries they cannot explain
      - Margery Plumwood (halfling, missing three fingers): *"Gardening accident. I've always been clumsy."*
      - Thom Ashwick (human, missing left ear and shoulder chunks): weeping silently, calls it a great massage
      - Brindel & Cor (gnome couple, missing toes and fingers): *"Best honeymoon ever! We've been here for weeks."*
      - Velvet (Eladrin, missing nose and patches of scalp): longest-term guest, months in; may be too far gone
   d. **Stopping the Mist** — required to fight the False Hydra effectively
      - Destroy steam pipes: AC 15, 20 HP each; immune to poison and psychic damage
      - Shut off pipes: DC 15 Investigation (find hidden valve behind loose marble panel in Steam Room) or DC 15 Thieves' Tools
      - Mist clears over 1d4 rounds; ranged attacks no longer at disadvantage
   e. **The False Hydra** — 3 heads, ~150 HP total (~50 HP per head); CR 8–10
      - Multiattack: three bite attacks (+8 to hit, reach 15 ft., 2d8+4 piercing)
      - Song of Forgetting: DC 16 Wisdom save or creature cannot target the hydra
      - Feed action: 2d10 damage to helpless NPC within reach, regain half as HP
      - Destroying a head lowers song DC (2 heads: DC 15; 1 head: DC 14; 0 heads: song stops)
      - Burrow: retreats underground between rooms; mist provides concealment
   f. **Post-Combat**
      - Guests freed from the song: most flee; Velvet may need Greater Restoration
      - Reception lockbox: 450 gp, jewelry worth 300 gp
      - Steam Room hoard: 1,200 gp, Ring of Mind Shielding, Potion of Greater Healing ×3, Oil of Etherealness, music box (plays hydra's song — DC 14 Wisdom save, single target)
      - Basement: Fey-Touched Map (partial Thither map), **Skabatha's Calling Card** — *"For services rendered in pest control. —S.N."* (She introduced this hydra deliberately)
   g. **Return to Caldrien** — unspecified compensation promised for disrupting the spa
      - Reward includes a Polymorph potion (useful for Orphanage infiltration)
