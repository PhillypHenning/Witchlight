# Little Treats Orphanage

## Overview

A seemingly quaint orphanage in Thither, run by a Night Hag the children call **Miss Able** (Er Able). Inside its walls is the **Toyhouse** — Skabatha Nightshade's personal home and workshop. The orphanage and the Toyhouse are the same location. The children here are terrified and do not show it.

---

## Exterior — Read-Aloud

> The fence appears before the building does — thick iron bars, taller than two men, barbed along the top at irregular intervals like something grown rather than forged. The air at the fence line carries something: not a sound, not a smell, more like a pressure, the feeling of a door just before it opens. Beyond the bars: a wide yard of packed dirt, empty. No movement. A scraggly kitchen garden along one side, untended, leaning toward the building. The building itself is red brick, stained grey-brown with decades of weather, the mortar lines dark with damp. Three stories. Narrow windows. The face of a building that has always looked abandoned even when it is occupied.

> On the upper floors, pale shapes press against the glass — faces, or the suggestion of faces, still as photographs. The yard between you and the door is completely still. The only way in is a pair of iron gates set into the center of the fence, and beyond them, set into the brick: a massive brown door, weathered and swollen-looking, with a Wind Up Key carved deep into its center. Large. Deliberate.

> There is no sound of children playing.

**On closer inspection** (if the party approaches the gate): a hand-painted sign is wired to the fence beside the gate — hanging crooked, listing left. In large letters: *Little Treats Orphanage.* Below, in smaller uneven lettering that is clearly a child's hand: *we go in bot dunt com out.*

**From the fence line**: upper-floor faces visible; brief flickers of movement behind ground-floor glass, a shape crossing a window and gone. Do not give them more than that.

## The Ward

The fence is magically warded — a trick Miss Able acquired from Endelyn Moongrave. Adults who attempt to pass through meet an invisible barrier; they feel nothing but cannot move forward. The ward reads form, not appearance.

| Who can enter | Notes |
|---|---|
| Children | Any creature genuinely a child in form passes freely |
| Skabatha | Absolute override |
| Those with Skabatha's permission | She can grant specific individuals passage |
| **Polymorph** | Changes true form — ward reads the polymorphed shape; entry permitted |
| **Wild Shape** | Uncertain — small harmless animal forms likely pass; predators likely do not |
| **Disguise Self / illusion magic** | Ward reads essence, not appearance; ignored |
| Adults | Cannot pass regardless of disguise or persuasion |

Miss Able knows when someone is at the fence. She may appear at a window to observe — she will not come to the fence herself.

---

## Miss Able (Er Able) — Night Hag

Grandmotherly in appearance: white hair pinned back, flour on her apron, a patient smile. She cannot be rattled and does not raise her voice. When she speaks to adults she is politely immovable. When she speaks to children she is warm in the way a trap is warm.

**Behavior:**
- Daytime: present, watchful, apparently hospitable — may call out through a window to adults at the fence with a polite explanation of the rules
- Night: patrols the dormitory alone, humming; children who are awake pretend to sleep; anyone found out of place is taken to the kitchen and does not return to their cot
- She commands 6–8 Dolgaunts (appear as toys); sends them first if she suspects intruders; acts personally only if they fail

**As a Combat Encounter:**
- Use Night Hag stat block; she will not fight in the open — she withdraws to the Toyhouse and activates all Dolgaunts simultaneously
- She does not fear death; she fears losing the children, which is the leverage

## The Children

Sixteen children are inside, plus Star. They range in age; the older ones have survived long enough to learn the rules and enforce them on the younger ones. This is the only social structure that matters.

**The Carnival Three**: the most recent arrivals, stolen approximately two weeks ago during the Witchlight Carnival. They are still learning the rules. They flinch at wrong things, reach for wrong objects, make noise at wrong moments. To the children who have been here longer, the Carnival Three are the most dangerous people in the building — not because of anything they intend, but because ignorance kills faster than malice here.

**The unspoken rules** (known by the long-timers, taught in whispers):
- Don't touch anything you don't recognize. Ask first.
- Don't be alone in the Playroom.
- Don't go near the coat rack in the Entry Hall.
- Don't look at the rocking horse.
- Stay in pairs after supper.
- If you hear humming and it isn't Miss Able, leave the room immediately.
- Never, under any circumstances, go near the trapdoor.

## Interior — The Survival Game

During the day the children move freely through the building, but the building is not safe. Skabatha's predators are stationed throughout, disguised as furniture and objects — some dormant, some actively hunting on a slow rotation. The Dolgaunts (disguised as assigned toys) are Miss Able's controlled assets; the others are Skabatha's, operating on instinct or standing instruction.

**Known disguised hazards** (expand as needed):
- **The Coat Rack** (Entry Hall) — Mimic; has been still long enough that most children treat the hall as a through-route only, never stopping
- **The Rocking Horse** (Playroom) — something occupies it; it rocks without wind; children do not approach it; no one remembers what happened to the last child who touched it
- **The Large Candle** (upper hallway) — a Will-o'-Wisp; it drifts slightly; children navigate by keeping it in peripheral vision, never looking directly at it
- **The Stuffed Bear** (dormitory, end cot) — unassigned, unclaimed; no child will take that cot; the bear has not moved in weeks; this is considered a good sign

The children do not fully understand what these things are — they know *don't* and they know *why* only in the vague way of learned fear. The Carnival Three do not yet know any of it.

**The rhythm of danger**: the predators are mostly passive during daylight. Miss Able does not want damaged children — they are less useful to Skabatha broken. The danger spikes at two moments: when a child is alone, and at night, when Miss Able's patrol suppresses one threat while the others stir.

## Key Areas

### The Grounds
- **The Yard**: packed dirt, empty; no child goes outside; the yard is not used
- **The Garden**: a scraggly kitchen garden along the east fence; untended, overgrown; whatever was planted here has been left to do what it wants; the plants lean toward the building
- **The Well**: covered; the children are forbidden from it; it does not draw water

### Entry Hall
- Warm, slightly wrong smell — baked sugar over something underneath
- A corkboard: children's names, weekly chore assignments, a column marked *Progress* with tally marks
- A chair for visitors; Miss Able stands, never sits when receiving guests

### Dormitory (2nd Floor)
- Twenty cots in rows; each child has a small wooden box for belongings and one assigned toy on their pillow
- The toys are Dolgaunts at rest — Miss Able calls this "keeping the nightmares away"
- At night, the toys watch; if a child leaves their cot the toy will follow

### The Playroom
- The nicest room in the building; Skabatha's gift to the children
- Enchanted toys throughout: puppets that move when unwatched, music boxes that play a beat too slow
- A large toy chest (locked, key held by Miss Able) — new Dolgaunts are stored here before assignment

### The Kitchen
- Miss Able's primary domain; the oven is always lit
- A large pot on the stove, always simmering; the smell is not entirely food
- A pantry with labeled jars; some labels are children's names; the contents vary

### The Toyhouse — Upper Floor (Decoy)
- A locked door at the top of the back staircase: Arcana DC 16 to identify the ward, Thieves' Tools DC 20 to bypass, Dispel Magic (DC 14) removes it for 10 minutes
- Miss Able does not enter unless summoned
- Inside: convincing props — a writing desk, an armchair, shelves of glass vials, a workbench with tools, a leather-bound diary on the desk
- On inspection: the vials are empty; the diary is blank pages; the tools leave no marks; nothing in the room has ever been used
- **Investigation or Arcana DC 14**: the room is a constructed set — Skabatha's scent is here but her presence is not; this has never been her true workspace
- A large ornate wardrobe dominates the far wall; it has **three keyholes**, each in the shape of a wind-up key slot, currently empty

### The Real Toyhouse — Behind the Wardrobe
- Inserting all three wind-up keys and turning them simultaneously opens the wardrobe — not to a hanging space but to a passage
- Skabatha's actual domain in Thither: her true workshop, her real diary, her personal collection of stolen children's memories in labeled vials
- The room is aware and will alert Skabatha directly (not Miss Able) if entered without her permission

### The Basement
- Trapdoor in the kitchen floor, beneath a hooked rug
- No light source functions here; permanent magical darkness throughout
- **Star** (Dirlagraun's displacer kitten) is caged here — alive, hungry, and angry
- Children who are taken by Miss Able at night come here; their current state varies
- Something else occupies the far end; it is chained; it has been here longer than Miss Able

## The Three Wind-Up Keys

Three physical keys, each bearing the same wind-up key decal carved into the orphanage's front door. They open the wardrobe in the decoy Toyhouse — all three must be inserted and turned simultaneously. Currently held across Thither:

| Key | Location |
|---|---|
| **First** | Cinderhold Prison |
| **Second** | Starved Goblin Casino |
| **Third** | Never Leave Spa |

The keys are not hidden — they are held by the owners of those locations, or kept somewhere significant within them. Skabatha distributed them deliberately; she trusts that no one can retrieve all three.

## Tempest

Bavlorna's hagspawn son is somewhere inside the orphanage (per Maurnu, Session 4). He is not a prisoner — his arrangement with Skabatha is unclear. He may be found in the upper floors or the Toyhouse. He will not be friendly to the party by default.
