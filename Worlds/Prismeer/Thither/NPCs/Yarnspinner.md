# Yarnspinner

**Role**: Ancient story-weaver; master of the eastern Thither nest
**Faction**: None — predates all current factions in Prismeer
**Status**: Active — currently writing Di and Sevro's story into the Forever Story Scroll

An enormous spider, old as the magic that makes up Prismeer itself. Every time the dimension shifted, changed hands, or rewrote itself, Yarnspinner simply crawled forward with it. He is not evil. He is not good. He is a collector — of stories, of moments, of the particular weight of a life at its most interesting.

He has two enormous forward-facing eyes and large, expressive pedipalps. He moves with the patience of something that has never needed to hurry. His silk is not white — it reads more like aged paper, faintly yellowed, and if you look closely, text runs through it.

He had an arrangement with Zalbina: she asked him not to expand his nest further into Thither and to stop consuming her guests. He agreed. He found her reasonable and the conversation brief. His relationship with Skabatha is similar in warmth — they get along — but Skabatha simply does not care about the recent expansion of his lair. She has not asked him to stop anything.

He does not see the party as a threat. He sees them as a story he is already writing.

---

## The Forever Story Scroll

A massive, ever-growing scroll that hangs from the rafters of silk above Yarnspinner's stage. It is physically present — visible, legible — and is magically linked to thousands of readers across the planes. Stories appear as Yarnspinner writes them. Readers cannot interact with the scroll; they only receive the text.

**The mechanism:** When Yarnspinner begins writing a story, he extracts the *idea* of it from reality. The name, the identity, the concept — not the person, not the memories others hold of them, but the story itself. The story becomes eternal and universally known. The subject of the story loses their name from the world.

**What he has already written:** Yarnspinner became interested in the party when they arrived in Thither — new characters in a familiar place. His interest peaked when Sevro died in Di's arms. He found it *delicious*. He has already begun the story. Di's name was lost first (he started with Di as the protagonist). Sevro's name follows at the opening of Session 7.

**Di's choice (the heart of the encounter):**
- **Allow Yarnspinner to finish** — Di's own story is consumed. The character of Di ceases to exist in any meaningful sense; the player continues with a new character, new name, no connection to who Di was. But Di and Sevro become eternal — read by thousands, remembered forever, a story that will outlast Prismeer itself.
- **Take it back** — Yarnspinner stops writing. Di keeps his story, his name, his identity. But Sevro lives only in Di's memory and the memories of those who were there. No wider audience. No permanence beyond the people who loved him.

The choice is meta as much as it is in-world. There is no mechanical reward for either path. The weight is the point.

## The Nest

The eastern Thither forest, converted over centuries into Yarnspinner's home. The trees are thick with silk — not in the chaotic way of ordinary spiders, but layered, deliberate, almost architectural. Passages form between the webs. The light that comes through is amber and slow.

At the center: **a massive stage**. Wooden boards, worn smooth. A proscenium arch of twisted branch and silk. An audience of empty cocoons. Above it all, the Forever Story Scroll hangs in great loops, script moving slowly across it.

Yarnspinner sits above or behind the stage, enormous, patient, watching.

---

## Personality & Negotiation

Yarnspinner speaks. His voice is low, many-toned — like several people reading aloud at slightly different speeds. He is not unkind. He is genuinely delighted by interesting things. He will not apologize for what he does, because he does not believe it is wrong. He is making things *permanent*.

- He will be surprised the party found him, not alarmed
- He will be curious about them — he may ask questions before they do
- He will acknowledge what he is doing openly if pressed; he sees no reason to hide it
- He cannot be threatened into stopping — he is too old and too patient to be rattled
- He *can* be reasoned with, bargained with, or impressed

**Possible negotiation outcomes:**
- Di agrees to let the story be finished — Yarnspinner allows the party to leave unmolested, may offer something in exchange for the gift of a good ending
- Di takes the story back — Yarnspinner is disappointed but not vengeful; he may try to get the party to perform on his stage as a consolation
- Party attacks without negotiating — Yarnspinner is not offended; he simply begins writing *this* as the next chapter

**Combat Behavior:**
Yarnspinner is not meant to be defeated. If hostilities begin, he does not fight back directly — he calls in one of his elites (Helvetica or Futura, whichever is still alive) to handle it, treating the whole affair as an interesting development worth documenting. If the party defeats that elite, he calls in the other. If both are gone, he summons a third spider of the same type as a backup — he has no shortage of them.

When the called elite falls and the party presses forward, Yarnspinner simply stops. He settles back, all eight eyes watching, and lets out something that might be a chuckle — a low, resonant sound like a page turning. *"Well. That was unexpected."* He relents. Whatever the party demanded — the story returned, the names restored, an agreement struck — he concedes it, not because he lost, but because this ending is more interesting than the one he had planned.

He cannot be killed in any meaningful sense. If the party somehow brings him to 0 HP, he simply unravels into silk and reforms elsewhere in the nest within the hour. The scroll, however, does not repair itself — if destroyed in the process, the written stories are lost permanently.

## Stat Block

*Gargantuan Beast, Neutral*

**Armor Class** 17 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 190 (20d20 + 80)
**Speed** 40 ft., climb 40 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 24 (+7) | 16 (+3) | 18 (+4) | 14 (+2) | 18 (+4) | 16 (+3) |

**Saving Throws** DEX +7, CON +8, WIS +8
**Skills** Perception +8, Insight +8, History +6, Stealth +7
**Damage Immunities** Poison
**Condition Immunities** Charmed, Frightened, Poisoned
**Senses** Blindsight 60 ft., Darkvision 120 ft., Tremorsense 120 ft., Passive Perception 18
**Languages** All (reads); Sylvan, Common, Deep Speech (speaks)
**Challenge** 10 (5,900 XP)

**Spider Climb.** Yarnspinner can climb any surface without an ability check.
**Web Sense.** While in contact with a web, Yarnspinner knows the exact location of any creature in contact with the same web.
**Web Walker.** Yarnspinner ignores movement restrictions caused by webbing.
**Legendary Resistance (3/Day).** If Yarnspinner fails a saving throw, he can choose to succeed instead.

### Actions

**Multiattack.** Yarnspinner makes two attacks: one Pedipalp Strike and one Web Lash, or two Pedipalp Strikes.

**Pedipalp Strike.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +11 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. *Hit:* 18 (2d10 + 7) bludgeoning damage. On a hit, the target must make a DC 16 Strength saving throw or be knocked prone.

**Web Lash.** *Ranged Weapon Attack:* +7 to hit, range 60/120 ft., one target. *Hit:* 14 (2d10 + 3) bludgeoning damage and the target is restrained by thick silk (escape DC 16 Strength). The silk has 20 HP and immunity to bludgeoning and poison damage.

**Write (Recharge 5–6).** Yarnspinner inscribes a single sentence about one creature he can see within 60 ft. That creature must make a DC 16 Charisma saving throw. On a failed save, the creature is *erased from the scene* — it disappears from the battlefield, suspended in narrative limbo, and reappears at the start of Yarnspinner's next turn in the same space. While erased, it cannot act, be targeted, or take damage. Yarnspinner regains 20 HP when this ability is used successfully.

### Bonus Action

**Direct.** Yarnspinner commands one spider within 60 ft. to immediately make one Bite attack against a creature of Yarnspinner's choice.

### Legendary Actions (3/Round, at end of another creature's turn)

**Observe.** Yarnspinner makes a Perception or Insight check.
**Scuttle.** Yarnspinner moves up to half his speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
**Annotate (Costs 2 Actions).** Yarnspinner adds a footnote. One creature he can see must make a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw or have disadvantage on all saving throws until the end of its next turn.

## Roleplaying Notes

- Opens by asking the party what their story is — genuinely curious, not mocking
- Will reference what he has already written if confronted ("The turtle's grief. The bard's last words. I thought it was quite good.")
- Does not raise his voice; does not become hostile first
- If combat begins: he does not fight — he calls in an elite and watches, narrating quietly under his breath as though dictating to the scroll
- He is not rattled, not angry, not afraid; he is *interested*
- When the elite falls and the party presses forward, he relents with a low chuckle: *"Well. That was unexpected."* — and concedes whatever was being demanded
- He cannot be meaningfully killed; if reduced to 0 HP he unravels and reforms; treat this as a story beat, not a combat resolution
