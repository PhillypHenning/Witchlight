# Warden's Pass

*Wondrous Item, Uncommon*

A heavy cloak of rough-woven canvas, dark grey and faintly stiff at the hem. The fabric smells of iron and ash. On the interior lining, embroidered in thread the color of dried blood: a six-sided die with a skull on the one-facing side — the mark of Skabatha's collection agents.

The cloak has no visible magical properties. It radiates faint transmutation magic if detected (DC 15 Arcana to identify without *Identify*). To casual inspection it appears to be a well-made, if grim, traveler's cloak.

---

## Property

While wearing this cloak, the bearer is recognized as an authorized agent by Cinderhold's entrance mechanisms. The iron gate opens for one creature wearing the cloak, granting uncontested passage. No check is required.

The cloak grants no protection, authority, or recognition once inside. It functions only at Cinderhold's exterior entrance.

**One cloak, one creature.** A single Warden's Pass admits one person. The party currently possesses one, recovered from a kidnapper killed during the ambush in Thither.

---

## DM Notes

- The party does not yet know this cloak is magical — they found it as loot on a dead attacker
- The hint that it matters: during Session 7, a cloaked figure is observed leaving Cinderhold unharmed — the emblem on their cloak visibly matches the one on this cloak
- Perception DC 13 to notice the emblem match during the observation scene
- This is the primary intended solution for Cinderhold infiltration; the party has one pass and must plan around that constraint
- Additional passes could theoretically be obtained from active kidnappers — a thread to return to when planning the Kidnapper file
